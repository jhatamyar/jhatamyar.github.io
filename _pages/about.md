I am a Research Fellow (equivalent to Assistant Professor) at the University of York Centre for Health Economics. My research sits at the intersection of causal inference and machine learning, health economics, and labour economics. I am particularly known for applying and extending machine learning methods to causal identification problems with applications to health and labour market questions. 

I received my PhD in Economics from the University of Miami in 2020, where I studied labour and gender.

Before economics, I had a previous life as a classical concert pianist — a training that, for better or worse, has left me with an unhealthy attention to detail.
